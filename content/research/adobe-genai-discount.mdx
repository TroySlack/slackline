## Business description

Adobe develops and provides software curated for content creation, photography, design, illustration, and video — applications that industry professionals rely on daily.

## Investment thesis

Despite a 35% downturn within the past year, Adobe has maintained highly profitable margins, operating with a steady **29.5% net profit margin**. Adobe generated **$9.85 billion** in FCF in FY2025, a 25% YoY increase from 2024. Considering strong financials, Adobe is incredibly discounted on a five-year investment horizon — most of the stock's downturn is due to fear around how generative AI will affect digital media creation.

Its high-margin cloud software, coupled with consistent revenue and aggressive stock buybacks, creates steady, long-term growth. Adobe will remain a dominant, wide-moat business with steady future growth. The current multiple compression prices in the worst-case AI disruption scenario that the underlying financials do not support, suggesting meaningful rerating potential over a five-year horizon.

## Moat source

High switching costs — both intellectual and monetary — are Adobe's strongest and widest moat. Professionals across all creative industries are deeply familiar with Adobe products. Switching off would require completely exiting Adobe's complex web of products, plus the time and cost it takes to learn new software. Furthermore, strong network effects from Adobe's web of digital media products, file compatibility, and hiring and training streams strengthen Adobe's grip on the creative world.

## Valuation

Adobe sits at a **14.75x P/E (TTM)** versus a five-year average of **39.4x**. EV/EBITDA has compressed in similar fashion, sitting at **10.87x** versus a five-year average of **25x**. While these multiples have compressed, annual revenue has increased steadily over the past five years, and gross margins have expanded slowly but steadily. This should scream "discount" to any outside observer — the market has priced in AI risk significantly, which is the main reason P/E and EV/EBITDA have compressed so heavily.

## Primary risk

Adobe's risks come from the threat of generative AI. New innovations in generative AI threaten the wide application of Adobe's products, threatening the dominance of the platform in creative software.

## Catalyst

The failure of generative AI to realize profits or capture meaningful market share within the creative digital media industry will reduce fears and uncertainty around Adobe. Recently, GenAI tools have failed to achieve durable adoption among the professional creative workflows that Adobe dominates, as seen with the recent shutdown of Sora AI. Furthermore, the 2025 MIT report *"The GenAI Divide: State of AI in Business 2025"* showed that 95% of companies utilizing GenAI are failing to achieve measurable profit or return on investment. Failure of GenAI to realize profits will restore confidence in human-driven digital media creation, reducing uncertainty around Adobe.

Adobe is already integrating AI into its software via its own generative AI product, **Firefly**. This strategic response allows Adobe to hedge against the risks GenAI brings while integrating and capitalizing on the synergies it offers.
