## Business description

Microsoft develops and sells software, cloud infrastructure, and productivity tools to businesses, governments, and consumers. Microsoft operates as a platform business, creating the underlying technology that corporations and businesses build on top of.

## Investment thesis

While Microsoft faces short-term margin pressure, the long-term case is anchored by an active and aggressive investing strategy in AI and cloud infrastructure. Microsoft has delivered revenue growth of 16% YoY and a ROIC of approximately 22%. Strong financials coupled with significant long-term investments in cloud networking, AI, and data center capacity will allow Microsoft to capture a meaningful portion of share in those emerging and quickly expanding markets.

EV/EBITDA and P/E ratios sit well below five-year and ten-year medians, presenting a well-discounted entry point into the company.

## Moat source

Multi-year government contracts within IT — protected by compliance certifications competitors struggle to replicate — combined with deeply embedded enterprise solutions, create cost-switching moats that are extremely dominant within the software industry.

## Valuation

As of May 2026, Microsoft's P/E and EV/EBITDA ratios sit at around **26.6x** and **16.3x** respectively, well below their ten-year medians of **31.8x** and **19.5x**. This discount comes entirely from the market's wariness of elevated capex spending, which significantly hurt the stock after Q1 2026 earnings. This presents a strong case for a long-term buy-and-hold position in Microsoft, as the market has unjustly penalized the company for short-term financing decisions, pricing in AI costs over AI returns.

## Primary risk

The cyclical nature of AI capital expenditures could compress FCF margins and ROIC before Azure can grow revenue enough to absorb the compression. Failure to realize synergies and revenue from recent capex spending will compress margins and destroy value creation on that invested capital.

## Catalyst

Increases in AI usage and demand for AI-driven solutions will allow Microsoft to utilize its recently acquired assets. Microsoft can lean on its operating power leverage if the need for AI and AI-related solutions continues to expand. Furthermore, if FCF margin holds or expands while Azure accelerates, this is a clear signal that Azure is creating value within Microsoft's cloud software solutions.

## Sell trigger

<SellTrigger>
Failure of Microsoft's long-term investments in AI and data center infrastructure to capture significant market share, or Azure and other cloud services growth falling below 20% for two consecutive quarters. Additionally, if ROIC falls toward 15% while WACC stays around 10%, this will trigger a sell, as it indicates the buffer that prevents the company from destroying value on invested capital is eroding.
</SellTrigger>
